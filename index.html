My name is Taylor Tan and I am currently a first year student at the University of California, Riverside. I am majoring in Linguistics, as I aspire to become a speech pathologist. My siblings and I struggled with a speech delay and this disability brought many challenges into our lives. Therefore, after overcoming my speech delay in sixth grade, I have been passionate about speech and helping those who are going through the similar challenges that I faced. I have volunteered with special needs kids, as well as taught some how to cheer. I am currently helping my younger sister improve her speech. As a Linguistics major, I am currently taking Spanish 001, and plan to take five more quarters of a language. I also plan to study abroad within my third year at the University of California, Riverside, as I want to adapt to multiple languages around the world. I am planning on starting my upper division Linguistics courses next year.

During high school, I was a part of both my high school's United Student Body Leadership Program and Cheerleading squad. Throughout Leadership, I was the performing arts commisioner, by which I recognized those performing arts students who went above and beyond. I was also the Class of 2020 School Site Council representative, in which I had the opportunity to voice my opinions on how to improve the student body. I was officer of multiple clubs, including Co-President and Founder of Interact Club, Vice President of Students for Kids, and United Club Council Chairperson of National Honors Society. Other clubs I joined include American Red Cross, Economics Club, Komforting Kids, Chinese Club, Polynesian Club, and Psychology Club. I've been recognized multiple times. In cheer, I was voted "JV Most Dedicated" my freshman year and "Varsity Most Dedicated" my senior year. In addition, I was awarded "Scholar Athlete" and "Student of the Month" twice.

I volunteered at Casa Colina my senior year to help students who had special needs. I would play games with them and assist them on their educational needs. I trained a special needs cheer team and taught them basic cheers and dances. I also helped them compete at cheer competitions. I worked at Panda Express last summer before school started as a part-time front of house store associate.
